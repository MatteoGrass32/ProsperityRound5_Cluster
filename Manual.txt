HINT : Timing, my dear. It is always about timing. Not in the dramatic sense that people mean when they say it. Not about being first or fastest. About understanding that similar things do not always happen at the same moment, and that the gap between them is often more useful than the things themselves.  Some products react quickly. Others take their time. That delay is not a flaw in the market. It is a feature, if you know how to read it. I have watched this play out across more decades than I care to count. One category moves. Another follows. The gap between them repeats, not perfectly, but consistently enough to be meaningful.  So ask yourself whether you can measure that lag. Is there evidence of a recurring timing gap between related goods? Does one category consistently lead while another consistently follows? If the answer is yes, and if that gap is stable enough to be relied upon, then you are no longer reacting to the market. You are anticipating it. Which is, I will tell you now, a considerably more comfortable place to trade from.  Recognizing structural similarity is one step, love. Recognizing sequence is another thing entirely. One tells you what is related. The other tells you what is coming.
